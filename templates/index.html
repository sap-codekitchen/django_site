What is CodeKitchen?

We are a student group in MIT's School of Architecture and Planning. Our mission is to improve digital literacy in environmental design and planning by encouraging students to share skills with each other. We aim to create a welcoming space for beginners. We believe that everyone has the capacity to be both a beginner and a teacher.

Over the past academic year, we held weekly informal meetups for architecture and planning students to discuss and learn about code. At these meetups, students learned how to build and design websites, how to write software and simple scripts, and built interfaces for generative art or 3D modeling. Students taught and learned HTML, CSS, Javascript, Python, Processing, C++, and Java.

Please join our mailing list for updates on our launch and activities for the 2014-2015 academic year. Email us and we'll add you to the mailing list.

Who are we?

In addition to the many students and volunteers who have attended sessions, we have a core group of 3 graduate students: Allegra Fonda-Bonardi, Sayjel Patel, and Ben Golder.

We are rethinking the goals of CodeKitchen

We are currently in the process of rethinking CodeKitchen's mission and activities to provide something more valuable to MIT and the broader community. As the academic year begins, we welcome your input and ideas. We are looking for people who can lend a hand or lead the effort (no programming experience is required to help run CodeKitchen).
