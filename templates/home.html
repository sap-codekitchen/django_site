CodeKitchen is a student group at MIT's School of Architecture and Planning . Our mission is to improve code literacy in architecture and planning. We do this in three ways: informal tutoring sessions, invited lectures, and an online catalogue of resources.

Every other week, our “Kitchens” provide an informal, beginner-friendly tutoring lab where students cook up projects and discussions. We encourage students to learn through teaching and to seek out help from their peers. We believe that everyone can be both a beginner and a teacher. Kitchens are preceded by lectures designed to spark debate about the role of software and technical expertise in architecture and planning.

In addition to tutoring and lectures, we curate an online catalogue of events, workshops, tutorials, online courses, articles, and fellowships related to the rapidly changing use of digital tools in architecture and planning.

Please join our mailing list for updates on our activities for the 2014-2015 academic year. Email us and we'll add you to the mailing list.
